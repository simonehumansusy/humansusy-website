AI is genuinely valuable. Most organisations are not set up to capture that value.

AI tools are creating real operational improvements and your competitors and clients are moving. The opportunity is concrete and worth pursuing. But for most organisations between 20 and 200 people, there is no structured approach to capture it. People are already using ChatGPT, Copilot, or other tools on their own initiative. There is no policy, no shared learning, no visibility on what is happening. What should be an organisational decision is unfolding as individual improvisation.

The question is not whether your organisation will adopt AI. It is whether that adoption happens by design or by accident.

Meanwhile, the people questions keep getting deferred. How does this change what we expect from our teams? What happens to the skills we spent years building? Who is thriving with these tools and who is quietly disengaging? These questions do not resolve themselves. Left unaddressed, they show up as adoption stalling, as uneven capability across teams, as the slow erosion of what made your organisation work well in the first place.

What is available externally does not help. Enterprise consultancies are too expensive and too generic. Technology vendors sell tools, not organisational change. AI literacy workshops teach button-clicking, not judgment. None of it is built for an organisation of your scale, where every person matters and the margin for getting it wrong is thin.

A human sustainable approach

I call this approach human sustainability: AI adoption designed so the technology strengthens what people can do, rather than quietly replacing or overloading it. It is grounded in research on how organisations and people actually work, and shaped by studying how real organisations have navigated AI adoption, what worked, what did not, and why. It is not a framework you need to learn before we start. It is the lens I bring to your situation.

How I work

Every engagement starts with understanding your situation: where your organisation is with AI, what your people are experiencing, and what you are actually trying to achieve. From that understanding, I design interventions that fit your scale, your culture, and your operational reality. The work draws on operational transformation, change management, and AI adoption methodology, and it always stays concrete.

Diagnosis

A structured assessment of where your organisation stands with AI adoption and what it means for your people, processes, and performance. You walk away with a clear picture and concrete recommendations.

Training

Building capability in your teams to work with AI effectively and sustainably. Not button-clicking workshops, but developing the judgment, boundaries, and practices that make adoption actually work.

Transformation

Strategy design, deployment support, and change management as one integrated engagement. From diagnostic findings through to adoption being operational, with a single point of accountability.
